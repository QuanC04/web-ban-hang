# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Bookora serves two primary groups. Buyers are Vietnamese readers browsing on mobile and desktop for curated books, comparing shop quality, adding items to cart quickly, and checking out with minimal friction. Sellers are small-to-medium book shops managing inventory, orders, promotions, and daily shop operations from a practical dashboard.

The buyer context is relaxed but purposeful: discovery, trust, pricing, and confidence matter more than novelty. The seller context is operational: they need readable dashboards, quick actions, and status visibility without visual clutter.

## Product Purpose
Bookora is a multi-shop book marketplace with a companion seller center. The storefront helps readers discover books, compare shops, and complete purchases smoothly. The seller surface helps shops run day-to-day commerce tasks such as monitoring orders, reviewing stock, and publishing products.

Success looks like this: buyers can move from discovery to checkout without confusion, and sellers can understand the state of their shop at a glance. The product should feel trustworthy, calm, and editorial rather than noisy or discount-driven.

## Brand Personality
Warm, curated, dependable.

The voice should feel welcoming and literate, with enough polish to make books feel considered rather than mass-listed. Copy should be concise, helpful, and plain. The buyer experience should feel like entering a modern bookstore with thoughtful curation. The seller experience should feel clear-headed and operational, not flashy.

## Anti-references
- Generic purple-gradient SaaS pages.
- Neon-dark ecommerce interfaces that feel like gaming dashboards.
- Card-on-card layouts with heavy chrome everywhere.
- Overdecorated bookstore aesthetics that imitate vintage paper in every component.
- Hype-heavy copy, fake urgency, and overly promotional microcopy.

## Design Principles
- Discovery should feel curated, not crowded. Show enough information to support choice, but keep visual noise low.
- Trust beats spectacle. Clear pricing, shop identity, and navigation matter more than decorative effects.
- Buyers browse, sellers operate. Storefront screens can be warmer and more atmospheric, while seller screens should stay sharper and more functional.
- Editorial warmth with product discipline. Use bookish tone and spacing without turning the interface into a themed poster.
- Actions should read immediately. Primary actions, status chips, and navigation states must be obvious at first glance.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Preserve readable text sizes on mobile, keep touch targets comfortable, and avoid motion that is required to understand content. Hover-only affordances should never hide critical actions. Color should support hierarchy, not be the only source of meaning for ratings, status, or form feedback.
